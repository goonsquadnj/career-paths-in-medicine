1. Private pre-health ecosystem
2. Private STEM / biomedical / health-tech fallback

4. Urban clinical or research access

This batch is not the final school list. It is a structured research pass.

# 1. Case Western Reserve University

## Preliminary category

Private pre-health / STEM / biomedical research / major hospital ecosystem

## Why it belongs in the candidate universe

Case Western Reserve belongs because it is one of the clearest examples of a private university whose pre-health value proposition is not just generic prestige. Its main strategic value is the combination of strong STEM/research orientation plus proximity to major Cleveland healthcare institutions.

CWRU says its pre-med students study in one of the country’s top medical communities, with nationally ranked hospitals within walking distance of campus. ([case.edu](https://case.edu/admission/academics/areas-study/pre-med))

CWRU’s medicine advising page says students can gain clinical exposure through proximity and association with University Hospitals, Cleveland Clinic, and Louis Stokes Cleveland VA Medical Center. ([case.edu](https://case.edu/studentlife/careercenter/graduate-and-professional-school-planning/pre-health-advising/medicine))

## Cost notes

Case Western’s 2026–27 first-year residential budget lists:

* Tuition: $71,410
* Fees: $696
* Matriculation fee: $760
* Housing: $11,688
* Meal plan: $8,600
* Books and supplies: $1,200
* Personal expenses: $1,350

The listed 2026–27 typical first-year residential cost is $95,704, excluding variable transportation. ([case.edu](https://case.edu/financialaid/undergraduates/estimated-costs-attendance-2026-27))

## Pre-health / medical ecosystem notes

CWRU’s Healthcare Careers advising page says Healthcare Careers advisors work with undergraduate students and alumni seeking careers in the health professions. ([case.edu](https://case.edu/studentlife/careercenter/graduate-and-professional-school-planning/pre-health-advising))

CWRU’s medicine page emphasizes proximity to University Hospitals, Cleveland Clinic, and the Cleveland VA for volunteering/clinical exposure. ([case.edu](https://case.edu/studentlife/careercenter/graduate-and-professional-school-planning/pre-health-advising/medicine))

## Strategic use case

Best used in the app as:

> Private pre-health/STEM ecosystem school with unusually strong hospital proximity.

Case Western is useful for comparing:

* Rutgers/Rowan/Stony Brook/Pitt vs. private health-sciences ecosystem
* Private sticker cost vs. clinical/research density
* Biomedical/STEM fallback vs. classic biology premed
* Strong pre-health environment vs. GPA and cost risk

## Watch-outs

* Very high sticker price unless aid changes the math.
* Strong STEM/science environment may still be rigorous and competitive.
* The app should not treat hospital proximity as automatically equal to easy clinical access.
* Need to compare actual net price and merit likelihood.

## Preliminary score notes

* Prestige band: 4
* Premed ecosystem: strong
* STEM/research fallback: strong
* Value: aid-dependent
* GPA/support risk: medium
* Fit question: Would Lucy like Cleveland, a research/STEM-heavy school, and an intense pre-health environment?

# 2. Boston University

## Preliminary category

Private urban research university / Boston medical ecosystem / broad pre-health path

## Why it belongs in the candidate universe

Boston University belongs because it represents a large private urban research university with strong pre-health advising and a major medical/research city context. It helps compare “private urban pre-health in a major medical city” against Drexel, Northeastern, Temple, Pitt, and Case Western.

BU’s Pre-Professional Advising Office says it advises students interested in medicine, dentistry, veterinary medicine, and other health-related fields on curriculum, professional school admission requirements, and the application process. ([bu.edu](https://www.bu.edu/caspreprofessional/))

BU’s pre-health advising staff page says pre-health advisors provide comprehensive guidance to BU undergraduates and alumni from undergraduate schools and colleges interested in medicine, dentistry, veterinary medicine, PA, nursing, optometry, podiatry, and other fields. ([bu.edu](https://www.bu.edu/prehealth/staff/))

## Cost notes

Boston University lists 2026–27 resident undergraduate cost of attendance at $98,419, including:

* Tuition: $73,024
* Fees: $1,570
* Housing and food: $20,740
* Books and supplies: $1,000
* Incidentals: $2,085

BU’s admissions page lists estimated billed expenses of $95,334 and total cost of attendance of $98,419 for 2026–27. ([bu.edu](https://www.bu.edu/finaid/undergraduate-students/undergraduate/), [bu.edu](https://www.bu.edu/admissions/tuition-aid/tuition/))

## Pre-health / medical ecosystem notes

BU has a dedicated Pre-Professional Advising Office and pre-health advisors. The office supports students and alumni pursuing a broad set of health-related professions. ([bu.edu](https://www.bu.edu/prehealth/staff/))

## Strategic use case

Best used in the app as:

> Private urban research/pre-health school in a major medical city, with high cost and broad option value.

BU is useful for comparing:

* Boston private urban research ecosystem vs. Philadelphia/Cleveland/Pittsburgh options
* Private urban pre-health vs. public urban Temple
* Large private university vs. smaller private schools like Lafayette/Lehigh/Villanova
* Boston brand/context vs. cost

## Watch-outs

* Very high sticker cost.
* Large urban university; advising and opportunity access may require self-direction.
* Boston medical ecosystem is valuable, but does not automatically make the school a better ROI than lower-cost options.
* Need to compare actual merit/need aid.

## Preliminary score notes

* Prestige band: 4
* Premed ecosystem: strong
* Urban clinical/research context: strong
* Value: aid-dependent
* GPA/support risk: medium
* Fit question: Does Lucy want a large urban private university?

# 3. Northeastern University

## Preliminary category

Private co-op / health sciences / STEM and health-tech fallback / Boston urban context

## Why it belongs in the candidate universe

Northeastern belongs because it has a distinctive co-op model, strong career orientation, Boston location, and formal PreMed/PreHealth Advising Program. It is useful for modeling a school that may be especially interesting if Lucy likes healthcare but also wants a practical STEM, health sciences, data, public health, or health-tech fallback.

Northeastern’s PreMed and PreHealth Advising Program says it provides individualized advising resources to students and alumni considering careers in health fields. ([northeastern.edu](https://undergraduate.northeastern.edu/prehealth/))

Northeastern’s catalog describes the PreMed and PreHealth Advising Program as a specialized advising unit under the Office of the Provost. ([catalog.northeastern.edu](https://catalog.northeastern.edu/undergraduate/university-academics/premedical-preprofessional-health-career-preparation/))

## Cost notes

Northeastern’s undergraduate catalog lists tuition at $33,995 per full semester, plus undergraduate student fee of $362.50 per full semester. ([catalog.northeastern.edu](https://catalog.northeastern.edu/undergraduate/expenses/tuition-room-board-fees-per-semester/))

Northeastern’s admissions page provides an estimated direct and indirect spring-semester cost of $47,159, including $33,995 tuition, $740 fees, $6,574 housing, $4,450 food, and estimated books/personal/transportation expenses. Doubling that spring-semester estimate gives a rough annualized figure around $94,318, but actual costs vary by program, campus pathway, co-op timing, housing, dining, and calendar. ([admissions.northeastern.edu](https://admissions.northeastern.edu/cost-financial-aid/))

## Pre-health / medical ecosystem notes

Northeastern has a formal PreMed and PreHealth Advising Program. The program is individualized and supports students/alumni considering healthcare careers. ([northeastern.edu](https://undergraduate.northeastern.edu/prehealth/))

Northeastern also has substantial health-sciences infrastructure through Bouvé College of Health Sciences, but the app should distinguish undergraduate pre-health advising from graduate health-professional programs.

## Strategic use case

Best used in the app as:

> Private co-op / health-tech / health-sciences option where career structure may matter more than classic premed prestige.

Northeastern is useful for comparing:

* Co-op model vs. traditional premed timeline
* Boston urban ecosystem vs. cost
* Health-tech/STEM fallback vs. pure premed
* Private career-oriented university vs. public value schools
* Northeastern vs. Drexel as co-op/urban practical pathways

## Watch-outs

* Very high sticker cost.
* Co-op timing may complicate traditional premed sequencing unless carefully planned.
* Strong career outcomes may reflect major mix and co-op, not necessarily med-school placement.
* Need to verify premed course sequencing and how co-op affects MCAT/application timing.

## Preliminary score notes

* Prestige band: 4
* Premed ecosystem: moderate-to-strong
* Health-tech/career fallback: strong
* Value: aid-dependent
* GPA/support risk: medium
* Fit question: Does Lucy like experiential learning/co-op and a Boston urban environment?

# 4. Villanova University

## Preliminary category

Private regional brand / Catholic university / pre-health advising / broad undergraduate fit option

## Why it belongs in the candidate universe

Villanova belongs because it is a well-known regional private university that many NJ families may consider. It provides a different strategic lane than BU, Case, or Northeastern: smaller than major urban research universities, stronger regional brand, Catholic/Augustinian identity, and formal health professions advising.

Villanova’s Health Professions Advising Office supports students and alumni planning, preparing, and applying to graduate programs in the health professions. ([villanova.edu](https://www.villanova.edu/university/liberal-arts-sciences/experience/ellis-center/health.html))

Villanova also has a Pre-Health Professions community page that describes resources for pre-medical, pre-dental, pre-veterinary, pre-PT/OT, and other pre-health pathways. ([villanova.edu](https://connections.villanova.edu/channels/pre-health-professions/))

## Cost notes

Villanova’s 2026–27 cost-of-attendance page lists traditional undergraduate full-time day commuter tuition at $72,990 and student services fee of $1,024 before other indirect costs. ([villanova.edu](https://www.villanova.edu/university/office-of-financial-assistance/financial-aid-process/cost-of-attendance.html))

Villanova’s bursar page lists 2026–27 undergraduate tuition at $72,990 for all colleges, with an undergraduate student services fee of $1,024. ([villanova.edu](https://www.villanova.edu/university/financial-affairs/bursar/tuition-and-fees.html))

## Pre-health / medical ecosystem notes

Villanova has a Health Professions Advising Office and resources for multiple pre-health pathways. ([villanova.edu](https://www.villanova.edu/university/liberal-arts-sciences/experience/ellis-center/health.html), [villanova.edu](https://connections.villanova.edu/channels/pre-health-professions/))

## Strategic use case

Best used in the app as:

> Private regional brand with formal pre-health advising and strong general undergraduate fit potential.

Villanova is useful for comparing:

* Regional private brand vs. NJ public value
* Private undergraduate environment vs. large public flagship
* Catholic/private campus fit vs. urban medical ecosystem
* General prestige/fit vs. specialized pre-health ecosystem

## Watch-outs

* Very high sticker price.
* Less obviously a dense medical-center ecosystem school than Case, Pitt, BU, Stony Brook, Temple, or Drexel.
* Need to understand merit/need aid and actual net price.
* Catholic/Augustinian environment may be a feature or a mismatch depending on Lucy.

## Preliminary score notes

* Prestige band: 3
* Premed ecosystem: moderate
* Regional brand: strong
* Value: aid-dependent
* Fit/support: potentially strong
* Fit question: Does Lucy like Villanova’s campus culture and size enough to justify higher cost?

# 5. Lehigh University

## Preliminary category

Private regional STEM/liberal arts / College of Health / pre-health advising / strong option-value school

## Why it belongs in the candidate universe

Lehigh belongs because it is a strong regional private university with a mix of engineering, liberal arts, business, and a College of Health. It is useful for testing the “private regional brand with STEM/health option value” lane.

Lehigh’s prospective pre-health page says first-year students attend pre-health group advising, create a Pre-Professional Portfolio, and leave group advising with a four-year academic plan for health-profession prerequisites. It also says the Health Professions Advisory Committee provides a committee letter for students applying to medical, dental, optometry, and podiatry programs. ([lehigh.edu](https://careercenter.lehigh.edu/content/prospective-pre-health-students))

Lehigh’s College of Health has a pre-health track for students preparing for medicine, dentistry, veterinary medicine, and other health professions. ([lehigh.edu](https://health.lehigh.edu/academic-programs/undergraduate-programs/pre-health-studies))

## Cost notes

Lehigh announced undergraduate tuition for 2026–27 of $69,420 and total cost of attendance of $89,820, including tuition, typical on-campus room and board, and fees. ([lehigh.edu](https://news.lehigh.edu/lehigh-announces-tuition-for-2026-27-academic-year))

Lehigh’s admissions page lists the 2026–27 total cost at $89,820, including tuition of $69,420, typical first-year housing of $11,470, full meal plan of $7,760, technology fee of $630, activity fee of $290, and wellness fee of $250. ([lehigh.edu](https://www2.lehigh.edu/admissions/tuition-affording-college))

## Pre-health / medical ecosystem notes

Lehigh’s pre-health advising structure includes group advising in the first year, a Pre-Professional Portfolio, a four-year prerequisite plan, and a Health Professions Advisory Committee committee-letter process. ([lehigh.edu](https://careercenter.lehigh.edu/content/prospective-pre-health-students))

## Strategic use case

Best used in the app as:

> Private regional STEM/health option-value school with formal pre-health advising, high cost, and strong fallback potential.

Lehigh is useful for comparing:

* Private regional brand vs. NJ public value
* STEM/business/health option value vs. pure premed
* Smaller university environment vs. large flagship
* College of Health / interdisciplinary health path vs. traditional biology premed

## Watch-outs

* Very high sticker cost.
* Less urban/medical-center-dense than Pitt, Temple, Drexel, Case, or BU.
* STEM rigor could create GPA risk depending major.
* Need to verify actual med-school outcomes and current student experience.

## Preliminary score notes

* Prestige band: 3
* Premed ecosystem: moderate-to-strong
* STEM/business fallback: strong
* Value: aid-dependent
* Fit/support: potentially strong
* Fit question: Does Lucy want a mid-sized private school with STEM/business/health breadth?

# 6. Lafayette College

## Preliminary category

Private liberal arts / smaller advising model / regional brand / health-professions advising

## Why it belongs in the candidate universe

Lafayette belongs because it represents a smaller liberal arts / undergraduate-focused private option. It is not the same strategic category as Case, BU, Northeastern, or Drexel. Its potential value is closer student-faculty environment, advising, and liberal arts fit.

Lafayette’s Health Professions Program says it provides guidance and resources to students at any stage of undergraduate study who are interested in health-profession careers. ([lafayette.edu](https://healthprofessions.lafayette.edu/))

Lafayette’s FAQ says the Health Professions Program offers group and individual advising, connects students with working health professionals interested in mentoring, and provides programs/workshops to help students discern health-professions interests, goals, and capabilities. ([lafayette.edu](https://healthprofessions.lafayette.edu/faqs/))

## Cost notes

Lafayette’s admissions page lists 2026–27 billed expenses as:

* Tuition: $70,486
* Matriculation fee: $750
* Student activity fee: $360
* Housing: $12,968
* Food: $7,928
* Total billed expenses: $92,492

The page also lists additional estimated non-billed expenses. ([lafayette.edu](https://admissions.lafayette.edu/college-costs/))

## Pre-health / medical ecosystem notes

Lafayette has a Health Professions Program supporting students interested in health-professions careers, with advising and mentoring resources. ([lafayette.edu](https://healthprofessions.lafayette.edu/), [lafayette.edu](https://healthprofessions.lafayette.edu/faqs/))

Lafayette also published a 2024 pre-application information session PDF reporting recent applicant metrics and an 84% medical-school acceptance rate for a recent applicant group. Treat this as school-reported and cohort-specific, not a universal guarantee. ([lafayette.edu](https://healthprofessions.lafayette.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/181/2024/10/FINAL-2024-Pre-App-info-session.pdf))

## Strategic use case

Best used in the app as:

> Smaller private liberal arts pre-health environment, where advising/fit may matter more than medical-center density.

Lafayette is useful for comparing:

* Liberal arts premed vs. large research-university premed
* Smaller advising model vs. broad hospital/research ecosystem
* Private regional brand vs. public value
* Fit/support vs. cost

## Watch-outs

* Very high sticker cost.
* Less obvious hospital/research density than Case, Pitt, BU, Stony Brook, Temple, or Drexel.
* School-reported med-school acceptance numbers require context; applicant self-selection and committee processes can affect rates.
* Need to compare actual net price and merit/need aid.

## Preliminary score notes

* Prestige band: 3
* Premed ecosystem: moderate
* Liberal arts fit/support: potentially strong
* Value: aid-dependent
* Clinical/research access: moderate/TBD
* Fit question: Would Lucy benefit from a smaller undergraduate-focused environment?

# 7. University of Rochester

## Preliminary category

Private research university / medical-center adjacency / REMS direct-med relevance / flexible curriculum

## Why it belongs in the candidate universe

University of Rochester belongs because it combines private research-university status, medical-center adjacency, health-professions advising, and a highly selective direct-medical pathway through REMS.

Rochester’s health-professions advising page says health professional advising helps prepare undergraduate students for graduate programs in medicine, dentistry, veterinary science/medicine, nursing, PA, pharmacy, allied health, and public health. ([rochester.edu](https://www.rochester.edu/college/ccas/handbook/health-professions-advising.html))

Rochester also says it has no formal pre-med or pre-health major, and that it is not necessary to be a biology or chemistry major to be accepted to a health professional graduate program. ([rochester.edu](https://www.sas.rochester.edu/bcs/undergraduate/premed.html))

## Cost notes

University of Rochester’s 2026–27 undergraduate cost page lists residential-student total cost of attendance at $96,022, including:

* Tuition: $71,750
* Mandatory fees: $1,622
* Housing: $12,822
* Food: $8,506
* Transportation: $300
* Personal expenses: $1,022

([rochester.edu](https://www.rochester.edu/financial-aid/undergraduate-tuition-expenses/))

Rochester’s 2026 announcement says undergraduate tuition will be $71,750 and housing and food will be $21,326 for 2026–27. ([rochester.edu](https://www.rochester.edu/newscenter/tuition-financial-aid-rates-set-for-2026-27-academic-year/))

## Pre-health / medical ecosystem notes

Rochester has Pre-Med and Allied Health Professions advising through the University’s health-professions advising resources. ([rochester.edu](https://www.rochester.edu/college/health/))

Rochester’s Health Committee Letter page says students applying to medical, dental, and veterinary schools can use the Health Committee Letter process and are encouraged to meet with health profession advisors in the Greene Center. ([rochester.edu](https://www.rochester.edu/college/health/apply/letter.html))

## Direct medical pathway notes

The Rochester Early Medical Scholars program is an eight-year BA/BS + MD program for outstanding undergraduates committed to medicine. Rochester says students accepted to REMS are admitted to the University’s School of Medicine and Dentistry once they successfully complete the bachelor’s degree and pre-med core courses. ([rochester.edu](https://admissions.rochester.edu/academics/rems/))

Rochester’s advising handbook says REMS is highly selective and limited to ten students per year. ([rochester.edu](https://www.rochester.edu/college/ccas/handbook/admissions-programs.html))

## Strategic use case

Best used in the app as:

> Private research/medical-center school with flexible pre-health path and highly selective REMS direct-med pathway.

Rochester is useful for comparing:

* Private research university vs. public value options
* Medical-center adjacency vs. classic campus fit
* Flexible curriculum/pre-health path vs. rigid premed assumptions
* REMS direct-med path vs. traditional premed path
* Strong health ecosystem vs. high cost

## Watch-outs

* Very high sticker cost unless aid changes the math.
* REMS is extremely selective and should be treated as a high-reach direct-med lane, not the base case.
* Need to verify actual med-school/research access for undergrads.
* Need to compare net price and merit/need aid.

## Preliminary score notes

* Prestige band: 4
* Premed ecosystem: strong
* Research/medical-center ecosystem: strong
* Value: aid-dependent
* Direct-med relevance: strong but high-reach
* Fit question: Does Lucy like Rochester’s flexible curriculum/research-campus vibe?

# What this batch teaches

## 1. Private does not mean one thing

These schools fall into different subtypes:

* Case Western: private pre-health/STEM/hospital-density school
* BU: large urban private research/pre-health school
* Northeastern: private co-op/career/health-tech school
* Villanova: regional private brand / Catholic / campus-fit school
* Lehigh: regional STEM/business/health option-value school
* Lafayette: smaller liberal arts advising/fit school
* Rochester: private research/medical-center + REMS direct-med school

## 2. The cost bar is very high

Most of these schools are around $90K–$100K sticker cost for 2026–27.

This means the app should ask:

> What does this school buy Lucy that Rutgers, TCNJ, Rowan, NJIT, Stevens, Pitt, Stony Brook, Temple, or Drexel do not?

## 3. The best private-school cases are specific, not generic

The strongest inclusion cases are not “private prestige.”

They are:

* Case Western: hospital/research density
* Northeastern: co-op/career/health-tech structure
* Rochester: REMS and medical-center/research ecosystem
* BU: Boston urban medical/research ecosystem
* Lehigh/Lafayette/Villanova: fit/support/regional-brand alternatives if net price is attractive

## 4. The app needs “use-case labels”

A single prestige or ROI score is not enough.

Recommended labels:

* Private pre-health ecosystem
* Private urban research
* Private co-op / health-tech
* Private regional brand
* Liberal arts pre-health
* Direct-med high-reach
* Aid-dependent option
