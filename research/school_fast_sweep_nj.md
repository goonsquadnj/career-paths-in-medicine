# 1. Rutgers University - New Brunswick

## Preliminary category

NJ public flagship / value premed anchor / large research university

## Why it belongs in the candidate universe

Rutgers New Brunswick is the obvious in-state public flagship baseline. For a New Jersey family considering medicine, it is one of the first “do not overpay for undergrad before med school” comparison points.

It also has a formal Health Professions Office serving Rutgers-New Brunswick students and alumni interested in medicine, dentistry, PA, podiatry, optometry, and other health professions.

## Cost notes

Rutgers’ 2025-26 cost page lists the following for in-state undergraduate students:

* Tuition: $14,933
* Fees: $3,891
* Room & board: $15,332
* Direct charges subtotal: $34,156
* Total estimated cost of attendance: $40,260 for on-campus students

For out-of-state undergraduate students, the same page lists total estimated cost of attendance at $61,635 for on-campus students.

Rutgers’ admissions page also lists 2025-26 New Brunswick undergraduate direct costs of $34,156 for New Jersey on-campus students and $54,981 for non-New Jersey on-campus students.

## Pre-health / medical ecosystem notes

Rutgers-New Brunswick has an official Health Professions Office advising pre-health students. The office says it serves Rutgers-New Brunswick students and alumni pursuing medicine, dentistry, PA, podiatry, optometry, and other health professions.

Rutgers’ ecosystem also includes Robert Wood Johnson Medical School in New Brunswick and Rutgers New Jersey Medical School in Newark. RWJMS describes itself as a comprehensive medical school focused on education, research, health care delivery, and community well-being.

Rutgers-New Brunswick also has special BA/MD-related pathways listed through the Health Professions Office, including BA/MD Robert Wood Johnson Medical School and BA/MD New Jersey Medical School.

Important nuance: Rutgers Honors College’s older 4+4 program page says that program is no longer accepting new applications as of fall 2022, so any Rutgers special pathway needs to be checked program-by-program rather than assumed active.

## Strategic use case

Best used in the app as:

> The NJ public flagship / value premed baseline.

Rutgers NB is useful for comparing:

* In-state public cost vs. private/reach cost
* Large research university vs. smaller undergraduate-focused college
* Strong ecosystem vs. large-school self-advocacy risk
* Premed value path vs. prestige-undergrad path

## Watch-outs

* Large school; student likely needs to self-advocate.
* Intro science courses may be competitive.
* Advising quality should be checked through current student feedback, but anecdotal sources should be labeled separately.
* Do not assume every BA/MD or early assurance program is currently active without verifying the specific page.

## Preliminary score notes

* Prestige band: 3
* Premed ecosystem: likely strong
* Value: strong for NJ resident
* GPA/support risk: medium, because large public university
* Fit question: Does Lucy want a large flagship environment?

## Data still needed

* College Scorecard graduation rate
* College Scorecard median debt
* College Scorecard earnings
* Common Data Set admissions profile
* Honors College merit/honors details
* Current student premed advising anecdotes, labeled anecdotal

# 2. The College of New Jersey

## Preliminary category

Smaller NJ public / value premed / BS-MD relevance

## Why it belongs in the candidate universe

TCNJ is useful because it represents a different kind of NJ public option than Rutgers: smaller, undergraduate-focused, and potentially more personal. It also has a known seven-year medical program with Rutgers New Jersey Medical School.

TCNJ’s Medical Careers Advisory Committee says it assists TCNJ students and alumni applying to medical school or other post-graduate health professional schools, and it explicitly says students from any major may be counseled for medical or dental school.

## Cost notes

TCNJ lists 2026-27 undergraduate direct costs as:

* New Jersey resident tuition and fees: $20,986
* New Jersey resident housing and food: $17,208
* TCNJ Book Bundle: $694
* Total direct cost per year: $38,888

For out-of-state students, TCNJ lists:

* Tuition and fees: $27,520
* Housing and food: $17,208
* Book Bundle: $694
* Total direct cost per year: $45,422

## Pre-health / medical ecosystem notes

TCNJ’s Medical Careers Advisory Committee is based in the Biology Department but serves students from any major who are interested in medical, osteopathic, or dental school.

TCNJ’s seven-year medical program is a joint TCNJ / Rutgers NJMS program. Students spend three years at TCNJ in an approved major and four years at Rutgers New Jersey Medical School.

TCNJ’s page says approved majors include biology, biomedical engineering, chemistry, computer science, economics, math, English, history, philosophy, physics, and Spanish.

The same TCNJ page notes that the seven-year program is not the only path to medical school, and says that over the last five years about 65% or more of traditional medical-school applicants from TCNJ have entered one or more U.S. medical schools. This is a school-reported figure and should be labeled as such.

## Strategic use case

Best used in the app as:

> Smaller NJ public value option with pre-health advising and a high-reach BS/MD lane.

TCNJ is useful for comparing:

* Smaller public vs. large flagship
* Public value vs. private undergraduate cost
* Traditional premed path vs. BS/MD path
* Undergraduate-focused environment vs. large research ecosystem

## Watch-outs

* Less massive research/hospital ecosystem than Rutgers, Pitt, Hopkins, etc.
* BS/MD path is highly selective and should not be treated as the base case.
* Need to verify current admissions competitiveness and continuation requirements for the seven-year program each cycle.

## Preliminary score notes

* Prestige band: 3
* Premed ecosystem: moderate-to-strong, especially for advising
* Value: strong for NJ resident
* GPA/support risk: possibly lower than large public, but still academically rigorous
* Fit question: Would Lucy prefer smaller classes and a more undergraduate-focused feel?

## Data still needed

* College Scorecard graduation rate
* College Scorecard median debt
* College Scorecard earnings
* Common Data Set admissions profile
* Merit scholarship details
* Current student feedback on pre-health advising and science grading culture

# 3. Rowan University

## Preliminary category

Practical NJ public / healthcare ecosystem / accelerated MD and DO relevance

## Why it belongs in the candidate universe

Rowan belongs because it is a New Jersey public option with a growing health-professions ecosystem. It is particularly relevant because Rowan has Cooper Medical School of Rowan University and Rowan-Virtua School of Osteopathic Medicine connections, making it more than just a generic regional public option.

Rowan’s Office of Pre-Health Programs says it supports students pursuing medicine, dentistry, occupational therapy, optometry, physical therapy, PA, podiatry, veterinary medicine, and other healthcare professions.

## Cost notes

Rowan’s 2025-26 cost of attendance page lists full-time undergraduate in-state total cost at $41,164. It includes:

* Tuition and fees: $17,428
* Books/materials/supplies/equipment: $1,330
* Housing: $11,550
* Food: $5,474
* Personal spending: $2,993
* Transportation: $2,345
* Federal loan fees: $44

For full-time undergraduate out-of-state students, Rowan lists total cost at $53,192.

## Pre-health / medical ecosystem notes

Rowan has an Office of Pre-Health Programs that provides advising, resources, and programming for health-professions students.

Rowan also lists several 7-year accelerated medical programs. Its 3+4 page says Rowan offers accelerated 7-year programs in medicine in partnership with Cooper Medical School of Rowan University and Rowan School of Osteopathic Medicine.

The same page says these programs are for incoming first-year students and can lead to BS/MD or BS/DO pathways, with students applying to medical school or veterinary school during junior year and entering professional school in their fourth year.

## Strategic use case

Best used in the app as:

> Practical NJ public health-professions ecosystem option, especially for MD/DO and broader healthcare exploration.

Rowan is useful for comparing:

* Rutgers/TCNJ vs. a more explicitly health-professions-oriented NJ public
* MD and DO pathway exposure
* South Jersey / Cooper ecosystem
* Public cost vs. private cost
* Traditional premed vs. accelerated-path option

## Watch-outs

* Need to separate undergraduate Rowan from Rowan’s medical schools.
* Need to verify how competitive the accelerated programs are and what continuation requirements apply.
* Need to compare research depth and clinical access against Rutgers, Pitt, Case, Hopkins, etc.

## Preliminary score notes

* Prestige band: 2 to 3
* Premed ecosystem: likely strong for practical health-professions pathways
* Value: likely strong for NJ resident, but cost similar to Rutgers/TCNJ depending housing/fees
* GPA/support risk: TBD
* Fit question: Is Lucy open to Rowan’s location and vibe?

## Data still needed

* College Scorecard graduation rate
* College Scorecard median debt
* College Scorecard earnings
* Common Data Set admissions profile
* Honors program details
* Detailed accelerated MD/DO continuation requirements
* Current student feedback on advising and science course experience

# 4. New Jersey Institute of Technology

## Preliminary category

NJ public STEM / health-tech fallback / pre-health honors / BS-MD relevance

## Why it belongs in the candidate universe

NJIT is important because it represents a different strategic lane: STEM-heavy, technology-oriented, potentially strong for biomedical engineering, bioinformatics, data/AI, and a health-tech fallback if Lucy pivots away from traditional medicine.

NJIT also has an NJIT/NJMS accelerated BS/MD program through the Albert Dorman Honors College, making it relevant to the high-reach direct-medicine lane.

## Cost notes

NJIT lists 2025-26 undergraduate tuition and fees as:

* New Jersey residents: $21,162
* Non-New Jersey residents: $39,912

For on-campus students, NJIT lists housing and food at $16,450 and additional indirect cost at $5,200. Books and supplies are listed at $2,900 for first-year and transfer non-architecture students.

## Pre-health / medical ecosystem notes

NJIT’s pre-health FAQ says pre-health advising is a cooperative effort between the student’s major advisor and the university pre-health advisor. It also says students applying to professional school may receive an NJIT-endorsed committee letter after an interview process.

NJIT lists pre-health majors including biology for medicine/dentistry/optometry, biology for physical therapy, bioinformatics, biomedical engineering, and biophysics.

NJIT’s accelerated BS/MD FAQ says the NJIT/NJMS program is a seven-year path: three years at NJIT followed by four years at Rutgers New Jersey Medical School.

The NJIT FAQ also provides useful competitiveness context for the 2022-23 cycle: 375 total applicants, 119 invited for ADHC interview, 75 selected by NJMS for interview, and 28 offered admission to the BS/MD program by NJMS.

## Strategic use case

Best used in the app as:

> NJ public STEM/health-tech option with a high-reach BS/MD lane.

NJIT is useful for comparing:

* Classic premed vs. STEM/engineering/health-tech path
* Public STEM ROI vs. private STEM schools like Stevens, Case, Northeastern
* Traditional application path vs. accelerated BS/MD
* Newark proximity to NJMS vs. actual undergraduate premed ecosystem

## Watch-outs

* STEM rigor may create GPA risk for a premed student.
* Need to understand whether Lucy likes tech/engineering/data or just “medicine.”
* The BS/MD program is very selective and should be treated as a reach lane, not the base case.
* Need to compare clinical/research access against Rutgers-Newark and Rutgers-New Brunswick.

## Preliminary score notes

* Prestige band: 2 to 3
* Premed ecosystem: moderate, potentially strong for honors/BS-MD
* Value: strong for NJ resident
* STEM fallback: strong
* GPA/support risk: potentially medium-high depending major
* Fit question: Does Lucy like a STEM/tech-heavy environment?

## Data still needed

* College Scorecard graduation rate
* College Scorecard median debt
* College Scorecard earnings
* Admissions profile
* Honors College details
* BS/MD current application and continuation requirements
* Current student feedback on pre-health advising and GPA culture

# 5. Rutgers University - Newark

## Preliminary category

Urban NJ public / Rutgers Health proximity / BA-MD direct-path relevance

## Why it belongs in the candidate universe

Rutgers-Newark belongs because it is a separate Rutgers campus with an urban setting and proximity to Rutgers New Jersey Medical School. It also has a seven-year BA/MD program with Rutgers NJMS.

## Cost notes

Rutgers’ 2025-26 admissions page lists Newark undergraduate direct costs for New Jersey residents as:

* Tuition: $14,933
* Fees: $3,179
* Room and board: $15,332
* Total direct cost for on-campus NJ residents: $33,444

For non-New Jersey residents, Rutgers-Newark on-campus direct cost is listed at $54,269.

## Pre-health / medical ecosystem notes

Rutgers-Newark’s Pre-Health Program says it is designed for students preparing for admission to allopathic and osteopathic medical schools, as well as dental, optometry, podiatry, and veterinary schools.

Rutgers-Newark’s BA/MD page says the seven-year BA/MD joint degree program is for high school seniors interested in a medical degree after the bachelor’s degree, and it is offered jointly by Rutgers School of Arts & Sciences-Newark and Rutgers New Jersey Medical School.

## Strategic use case

Best used in the app as:

> Urban Rutgers public option with NJMS proximity and direct BA/MD relevance.

Rutgers-Newark is useful for comparing:

* Rutgers-Newark vs. Rutgers-New Brunswick
* Urban clinical proximity vs. flagship campus breadth
* BA/MD direct path vs. traditional premed
* Public cost vs. private medical-path options

## Watch-outs

* Need to distinguish Rutgers-Newark from Rutgers-New Brunswick in the UI.
* Need to verify campus fit, safety/location comfort, and student support.
* BA/MD admission is not the same as normal Rutgers-Newark admission and should be treated as a separate high-reach process.

## Preliminary score notes

* Prestige band: 2 to 3
* Premed ecosystem: likely strong for NJMS proximity
* Value: strong for NJ resident
* Urban clinical access: potentially strong

# 6. Stevens Institute of Technology

## Preliminary category

Private STEM / high-ROI engineering brand / health-tech fallback / accelerated pre-med option

## Why it belongs in the candidate universe

Stevens is important because it represents a high-ROI STEM/private school lane rather than a classic premed lane. It may be especially relevant if Lucy likes biomedical engineering, data, AI, health tech, or a strong non-med fallback.

Stevens also has a Pre-Health Advisory Program and an accelerated pre-medicine program paired with Rutgers New Jersey Medical School.

## Cost notes

Stevens lists 2026-27 undergraduate cost of attendance for students residing on campus as:

* Tuition and fees: $68,230
* Loan fees: $70
* Housing and meals: $20,744
* Books and supplies: $1,200
* Miscellaneous: $1,050
* Total cost of attendance: $91,294

For 2026-27 commuting students, Stevens lists total cost of attendance at $73,320.

Stevens’ own cost page also says it ranks strongly for ROI and specifically states that PayScale ranked Stevens No. 18 nationally for best value based on ROI. Treat this as a school-reported marketing claim unless separately verified against PayScale.

## Pre-health / medical ecosystem notes

Stevens’ Pre-Health Advisory Program says it offers a community of advisors and services to students in any major interested in health professions after graduation.

Stevens’ pre-health advisory committee page says the Director of Pre-Health Advising works one-on-one with students considering health professions, helps with prerequisites and program selection, and supports the application process.

Stevens’ accelerated programs page says its Accelerated Pre-Medicine program is paired with Rutgers New Jersey Medical School, and that students apply to both Stevens and the medical school as high-school seniors.

## Strategic use case

Best used in the app as:

> STEM/health-tech fallback and private ROI case, not the default classic premed case.

Stevens is useful for comparing:

* STEM fallback value vs. traditional premed ecosystem
* Private cost vs. public value
* High salary/ROI school-level outcomes vs. premed GPA risk
* Engineering/tech identity vs. patient-care identity
* Accelerated pre-med option vs. traditional path

## Watch-outs

* Very high sticker price unless merit/aid changes the math.
* STEM rigor may be a GPA risk for a premed student.
* Strong ROI may reflect engineering/computing salary outcomes more than medical-school placement.
* Need to separate “great STEM career outcomes” from “best premed launchpad.”

## Preliminary score notes

* Prestige band: 3
* Premed ecosystem: moderate
* STEM fallback: strong
* Value: depends heavily on aid/merit
* GPA/support risk: potentially medium-high
* Fit question: Does Lucy like tech/engineering enough for this to be a feature, not a distraction?

## Data still needed

* College Scorecard graduation rate
* College Scorecard median debt
* College Scorecard earnings
* PayScale/Georgetown ROI verification
* Current student feedback on pre-health advising
* Accelerated pre-med current admissions details
* Merit aid distribution

# 7. Seton Hall University

## Preliminary category

Private NJ Catholic university / pre-health advising / joint MD interview pathway / merit-sensitive option

## Why it belongs in the candidate universe

Seton Hall belongs because it is a nearby private university with formal pre-health advising and a joint Bachelor’s/M.D. program with Hackensack Meridian School of Medicine. It also helps illustrate the difference between true guaranteed admission and guaranteed interview-style pathways.

## Cost notes

Seton Hall’s bursar page lists Academic Year 2027 undergraduate full-time tuition at $26,920 per semester, or $53,840 for two semesters before required fees, housing, food, books, and other expenses. It also lists a full-time University Fee of $1,145 per semester and a Mobile Computing Fee of $335 per semester.

Seton Hall’s affordability page says 98% of students receive financial aid, students receive nearly $25,000 per year in grants and scholarships on average, and all students are automatically considered for a University Scholarship at admission based on academic record. Treat this as school-reported aid positioning; actual net price must be modeled using the net price calculator and/or award letter.

## Pre-health / medical ecosystem notes

Seton Hall’s Pre-Professional Advising Center says it supports students and alumni pursuing legal and health professions and provides opportunities to explore, prepare, and apply to health professions.

The same page says students are encouraged to connect with P-PAC in the first semester of freshman year and continue throughout undergrad. It lists 1:1 advising, small group advising, workshops, academic success coaching, application preparation, and exploration opportunities.

Seton Hall’s pre-medical page says the Pre-Professional Advising Center can prepare a Committee Letter and that students should build relationships and secure letters from science faculty, non-science faculty, clinical supervisors, and extracurricular/research supervisors.

Seton Hall’s Joint Bachelor’s/M.D. page says the program allows students to complete a bachelor’s degree in any major while taking required prerequisites for Hackensack Meridian School of Medicine. Importantly, the page says the program guarantees an interview with the School of Medicine if continuation requirements are met, but does not guarantee admission to the medical school.

The same page lists admission minimums of 3.6 unweighted GPA, 30 ACT, or 1400 SAT.

## Strategic use case

Best used in the app as:

> Nearby private pre-health option with formal advising and a guaranteed-interview MD pathway, highly dependent on net cost.

Seton Hall is useful for comparing:

* Private merit-aid path vs. NJ public cost
* Guaranteed interview vs. guaranteed admission
* Smaller/private advising model vs. large public advising model
* Hackensack Meridian connection vs. Rutgers/Rowan ecosystems

## Watch-outs

* Sticker cost is high relative to NJ publics.
* Joint MD path is not guaranteed med-school admission.
* Need to verify actual net price and merit award for Lucy specifically.
* Need to understand whether the Catholic/private university environment fits Lucy.

## Preliminary score notes

* Prestige band: 2 to 3
* Premed ecosystem: moderate-to-strong advising structure
* Value: depends heavily on merit/aid
* Direct-path relevance: yes, but not a guaranteed admission program
* Fit question: Would Lucy like Seton Hall’s size, location, and Catholic/private environment?

## Data still needed

* Full cost of attendance, including housing/food/books/fees
* College Scorecard graduation rate
* College Scorecard median debt
* College Scorecard earnings
* Merit scholarship ranges
* Hackensack Meridian pathway continuation details
* Current student feedback on pre-health advising
