# 1. University of Pittsburgh

## Preliminary category

Urban research university / health sciences ecosystem / UPMC-adjacent pre-health option

## Why it belongs in the candidate universe

Pitt belongs because it is one of the most relevant regional “leave NJ for a serious health sciences ecosystem” options. It is a large research university in a city with a major academic medical ecosystem, and it has formal pre-health advising.

Pitt’s Pre-Health Resource Center says it focuses on Pitt’s undergraduate pre-health population and provides in-person advising, online tools, career resources, and pre-health workshops.

## Cost notes

For 2025–26 at the Pittsburgh campus, Pitt lists Dietrich School of Arts and Sciences tuition at $20,966 for Pennsylvania residents and $41,662 for out-of-state residents. Mandatory fees are $1,770, and “Other Estimated Expenses” for living on or off campus total $19,176. That implies a rough annual cost of about $41,912 for PA-resident Arts & Sciences students and $62,608 for out-of-state Arts & Sciences students before any aid. Some schools at Pitt, including Nursing, Health and Rehabilitation Sciences, Pharmacy, Business, Engineering, and Computing, have higher tuition.

## Pre-health / medical ecosystem notes

Pitt’s Pre-Health Resource Center has a dedicated advising staff and offers advising for students pursuing advanced education in health careers.

Pitt also has a School of Medicine Guaranteed Admission Program. The program is for high-school seniors admitted to Pitt undergrad who are interested in Pitt’s medical school; applicants must apply early, meet very high academic criteria, and selected students must maintain a 3.70 cumulative GPA and 3.70 BCPM GPA, complete the degree within four years, and score at least 517 on the MCAT within three attempts.

## Strategic use case

Best used in the app as:

> Urban health-sciences research university with strong pre-health ecosystem and a high-reach medical guarantee lane.

Pitt is useful for comparing:

* Rutgers/Rowan health ecosystem vs. Pitt/UPMC ecosystem
* NJ in-state public cost vs. out-of-state public cost
* Traditional premed path vs. guaranteed-admission medical pathway
* Strong urban clinical/research exposure vs. cost

## Watch-outs

* For a NJ family, Pitt is an out-of-state public and may be expensive without merit or aid.
* The Medicine GAP is extremely high-reach and has demanding continuation requirements.
* Pitt’s value depends heavily on whether Lucy benefits from the health-sciences ecosystem enough to justify higher cost than NJ publics.

## Preliminary score notes

* Prestige band: 3 to 4
* Premed ecosystem: strong
* Value: depends on aid; weaker than NJ publics at full OOS price
* Clinical/research access: strong
* Fit question: Would Lucy like an urban campus with a major health system nearby?

# 2. Penn State University Park

## Preliminary category

Large regional flagship / broad option value / traditional pre-health path

## Why it belongs in the candidate universe

Penn State belongs because it is a major regional flagship with broad alumni recognition, many majors, and a large-campus experience. It is useful as a comparison point against Rutgers, Pitt, Maryland, and Delaware.

Penn State’s Eberly College of Science has prehealth advising resources, and Penn State says prehealth advisers offer individual appointments covering academic planning, application guidance, health-care career exploration, volunteering/shadowing, test prep, research opportunities, and application resources.

## Cost notes

For University Park, Penn State lists 2026 fall semester academic-year full-time tuition for lower-division Pennsylvania residents at $20,878 and lower-division non-Pennsylvania residents at $44,574. Upper-division tuition varies by major; for example, nonresident junior/senior Science, IST, and EMS programs are listed at $49,616, while nonresident Business, Engineering, and Nursing are listed at $52,656.

Penn State separately lists cost-of-attendance elements per semester. For an on-campus student without dependents, food is $3,170, housing is $4,503, books/materials are $645, transportation is $1,122, and miscellaneous is $1,556 per semester. These are in addition to tuition.

## Pre-health / medical ecosystem notes

Penn State has formal prehealth advising through Eberly College of Science, including a prehealth advising SharePoint and individual advising. The individual advising page says advisers cover academic planning, application guidance, career exploration, volunteering/shadowing, test preparation, research opportunities, and professional-school application resources.

## Strategic use case

Best used in the app as:

> Large regional flagship with broad option value, not necessarily a specialized premed pick.

Penn State is useful for comparing:

* Rutgers vs. another large flagship
* Brand/alumni network vs. cost
* Big-campus fit vs. smaller TCNJ-style fit
* Broad fallback value if Lucy pivots away from medicine

## Watch-outs

* For a NJ family, Penn State University Park is likely expensive as an out-of-state public.
* The main campus is not an urban clinical campus in the same way as Pitt, Temple, Drexel, or Rutgers-Newark.
* Strong option value may matter more than specialized premed advantage.

## Preliminary score notes

* Prestige band: 3
* Premed ecosystem: moderate-to-strong
* Value: weak at full OOS price compared with NJ publics
* Broad fallback: strong
* Fit question: Does Lucy want a large flagship college-town experience?

# 3. University of Maryland - College Park

## Preliminary category

Strong regional public / STEM + pre-health / large research university

## Why it belongs in the candidate universe

Maryland belongs because it is a strong regional public research university with broad academic reputation, STEM strength, and formal pre-health advising.

UMD’s Reed-Yorke Health Professions Advising Office says it serves all University of Maryland students and alumni, regardless of major, who are interested in the health professions. The office reports advising 6,000 students per year across 11 pre-health disciplines and 600+ applications submitted per year.

## Cost notes

For 2026–27, UMD lists undergraduate Maryland-resident on-campus estimated cost at $34,268. For non-Maryland residents, UMD lists on-campus estimated cost at $65,520. This includes tuition, mandatory fees, on-campus housing, food, books/supplies, transportation, and personal costs.

## Pre-health / medical ecosystem notes

UMD’s pre-health office is housed in the College of Computer, Mathematical, and Natural Sciences but serves all UMD students and alumni. The office offers appointments and drop-ins, with advising organized around the student’s path.

## Strategic use case

Best used in the app as:

> Strong regional public research university with STEM/pre-health breadth, but expensive for out-of-state students.

Maryland is useful for comparing:

* Rutgers vs. another strong public research university
* STEM/research breadth vs. cost
* College Park/DC-area opportunity vs. NJ in-state value
* Prestige/academic reputation vs. premed ROI

## Watch-outs

* For a NJ family, the nonresident cost is high.
* UMD may be a great school but not necessarily a better premed ROI than Rutgers unless Lucy has a specific fit or scholarship reason.
* Need to compare actual merit possibilities and honors options.

## Preliminary score notes

* Prestige band: 3 to 4
* Premed ecosystem: strong
* Value: weak at full OOS price
* STEM/research fallback: strong
* Fit question: Does Lucy like a large research university and College Park/DC-adjacent environment?

# 4. University of Delaware

## Preliminary category

Regional public / health sciences / approachable campus / OOS comparison point

## Why it belongs in the candidate universe

Delaware belongs because it is a familiar regional public option for NJ families: not too far, more campus-contained than some urban schools, and potentially attractive if the fit is better than Rutgers or TCNJ.

UD has a Center for Health Profession Studies that supports students preparing for health-professions careers such as medicine, dentistry, podiatry, PA, pharmacy, optometry, and more.

## Cost notes

For 2026–27 Newark Campus undergraduates, UD lists on-campus billable academic-year total at $34,904 for Delaware residents and $61,634 for nonresidents. When non-billable estimated costs are included, the total estimated yearly cost of attendance is $39,154 for Delaware residents and $65,884 for nonresidents. UD also notes that students in Business, Engineering, Music, Honors College, and Nursing may be assessed differential charges.

UD’s cost page lists specific differential charges, including charges for Nursing, Business, Engineering, Honors College, and Music for students admitted starting Fall 2025.

## Pre-health / medical ecosystem notes

UD’s Center for Health Profession Studies provides advising and support for pre-health students and alumni. Its advising page lists staff and references the Medical Scholars Program and Health Professions Evaluation Committee.

## Strategic use case

Best used in the app as:

> Regional public campus-fit option with pre-health advising, but expensive as a nonresident unless aid changes the math.

Delaware is useful for comparing:

* Rutgers/TCNJ vs. a regional out-of-state public
* Campus vibe vs. cost
* Health sciences/nursing/PA-adjacent exploration
* OOS cost discipline

## Watch-outs

* For a NJ family, Delaware’s nonresident cost is in private-school territory before aid.
* Honors, Nursing, Engineering, and other differential charges need to be modeled separately.
* Need to verify merit-aid likelihood for Lucy’s profile.

## Preliminary score notes

* Prestige band: 3
* Premed ecosystem: moderate-to-strong
* Value: weak at full OOS cost; potentially better with aid
* Fit: potentially strong if Lucy wants a classic campus feel
* Fit question: Is UD meaningfully better for Lucy than NJ options, or merely familiar/comfortable?

# 5. Stony Brook University

## Preliminary category

SUNY research university / strong science / Stony Brook Medicine ecosystem

## Why it belongs in the candidate universe

Stony Brook belongs because it is a strong science/research-oriented SUNY with a medical center ecosystem. It is relevant for a student exploring premed, science, health sciences, research, or clinical exposure.

Stony Brook’s undergraduate advising site defines pre-health broadly, covering pre-med, pre-dental, pre-vet, pre-PA, pre-PT, pre-nursing, and other paths.

## Cost notes

For 2026–27, Stony Brook lists estimated yearly undergraduate full-time cost of attendance at $34,819 for New York State residents and $58,799 for out-of-state residents. On-campus direct costs are $30,671 for NY residents and $54,651 for out-of-state residents.

## Pre-health / medical ecosystem notes

Stony Brook has dedicated pre-health advising through undergraduate advising. Its health sciences advising page also directs students planning for medical, dental, or nursing school to the Pre-Health Advising Center.

## Strategic use case

Best used in the app as:

> Strong SUNY science/research and medical-center ecosystem option.

Stony Brook is useful for comparing:

* Rutgers vs. a science-heavy SUNY research university
* NJ in-state value vs. SUNY OOS cost
* Clinical/research ecosystem vs. campus fit
* Strong science environment vs. GPA risk

## Watch-outs

* For a NJ family, Stony Brook is an out-of-state public.
* It may be a strong science/premed environment, but that can also mean competitive science courses.
* Need to compare distance/campus fit and merit aid.

## Preliminary score notes

* Prestige band: 3
* Premed ecosystem: strong
* Value: moderate at OOS price; better than some OOS publics but still above NJ options
* Research/clinical access: strong
* Fit question: Would Lucy like Stony Brook’s campus/location and science-heavy environment?

# 6. Binghamton University

## Preliminary category

SUNY value / strong academics / traditional pre-health path

## Why it belongs in the candidate universe

Binghamton belongs because it is a strong regional public academic option with a reputation for value, especially for New York residents, and a formal pre-health advising office.

Binghamton’s Pre-Health Professions Advising Office says it was created in 1966 and supports students pursuing medicine, dentistry, pharmacy, and other health careers. It provides curricular advising, credentialing, programming, special programs, and general advising.

## Cost notes

For 2026–27, Binghamton lists estimated undergraduate cost of attendance at $32,746 for New York State residents living on or off campus and $55,716 for nonresidents living on or off campus. This includes tuition, fees, books, housing/food, transportation, and personal expenses.

## Pre-health / medical ecosystem notes

Binghamton has a dedicated Pre-Health Professions Advising Office. It offers pre-health appointments and peer advising, and the office supports students across health-professions tracks.

## Strategic use case

Best used in the app as:

> Strong regional public academics/value option, but not obviously better for NJ premed ROI unless fit/aid is strong.

Binghamton is useful for comparing:

* Rutgers/TCNJ vs. SUNY academic value
* Strong academics vs. clinical ecosystem depth
* OOS public value vs. NJ in-state value
* Traditional prehealth advising model

## Watch-outs

* For a NJ family, Binghamton’s OOS cost is meaningfully higher than NJ publics.
* It may have strong academics, but it is less obviously an urban clinical ecosystem than Pitt, Temple, Drexel, Rutgers-Newark, or Stony Brook.
* Need to compare actual premed outcomes and clinical access.

## Preliminary score notes

* Prestige band: 3
* Premed ecosystem: moderate-to-strong
* Value: good for NY residents, mixed for NJ at OOS price
* Clinical access: TBD
* Fit question: Would Lucy prefer Binghamton’s academic/campus environment over Rutgers or TCNJ?

# 7. Temple University

## Preliminary category

Urban public / Philadelphia clinical exposure / Temple Health / special pre-health programs

## Why it belongs in the candidate universe

Temple belongs because it offers an urban Philadelphia environment, a major health system, and formal pre-professional health advising. It is one of the best schools in this batch for illustrating “urban clinical exposure” without jumping to elite-private pricing.

Temple’s Pre-Professional Health Advising office serves current Temple students and alumni interested in health professions. The office supplements academic advising in the student’s primary college.

## Cost notes

Temple’s undergraduate tuition varies by school or college. For 2025–26, Temple lists full-time undergraduate annual tuition for the College of Liberal Arts at $19,608 in-state and $35,232 out-of-state, the College of Science and Technology at $23,856 in-state and $39,480 out-of-state, and the Barnett College of Public Health at $23,448 in-state and $40,752 out-of-state. These figures are tuition rates and do not include the full cost of attendance.

## Pre-health / medical ecosystem notes

Temple’s Pre-Professional Health Advising office has four pre-health advisors and advising is organized by completed credits.

Temple also has a Pre-Med Health Scholars Program with a linkage agreement to the Lewis Katz School of Medicine. Starting with the entering undergraduate class of 2025, interested students begin the application process in their first year at Temple. The program requires a 3.6 cumulative GPA, 3.6 science GPA, MCAT minimum composite 509 with no section below 126, and volunteer exposure, service, and research.

Temple’s special programs page also lists accelerated programs for dentistry, pharmacy, podiatry, PA, and physical therapy.

## Strategic use case

Best used in the app as:

> Urban clinical exposure and Temple Health pathway option, with multiple health-profession accelerated/linkage programs.

Temple is useful for comparing:

* Urban clinical exposure vs. suburban/campus feel
* Temple vs. Drexel in Philadelphia
* Temple vs. Rutgers-Newark/Rowan for public health-system proximity
* MD linkage vs. other health-profession accelerated paths

## Watch-outs

* Full cost must be modeled carefully because tuition varies by college and residency.
* Campus/location fit matters.
* Linkage/special programs have requirements and are not the same as a guaranteed free pass into professional school.

## Preliminary score notes

* Prestige band: 3
* Premed ecosystem: strong for advising and urban clinical context
* Value: depends on residency, aid, and program
* Clinical access: strong
* Fit question: Does Lucy want an urban Philadelphia campus?

# 8. Drexel University

## Preliminary category

Private urban co-op / pre-health / BS+MD early assurance / healthcare-flex option

## Why it belongs in the candidate universe

Drexel belongs because it represents a different kind of private urban pathway: co-op, pre-health advising, Philadelphia clinical/research environment, and an active BA/BS+MD Early Assurance Program.

Drexel’s preprofessional programs page says there is no single undergraduate path to professional school and that a future medical student may major in many areas, including humanities and engineering. It also says Steinbright can help pre-health students with course selection, career information, and the application process.

## Cost notes

Drexel’s undergraduate financing page lists estimated first-year costs for 2026–27 of $63,078 tuition, $2,420 fees, and $19,398 housing and food based on three quarters. That is a high sticker price before aid, books, personal expenses, and other assumptions.

Drexel Central’s undergraduate program-cost page also lists 2026–27 tuition at $15,459 per quarter and room/board at $23,832 for four quarters for the program-cost view shown there; Drexel’s quarter/co-op structure means the app needs to be careful about which program/calendar is being modeled.

## Pre-health / medical ecosystem notes

Drexel’s preprofessional page says students may pursue accelerated degree programs, early assurance or combined degree programs, and preprofessional advising through the Steinbright Career Development Center.

Drexel’s BA/BS+MD Early Assurance Program is a 4+4 combined program allowing students to gain early acceptance into undergrad and medical school at the same time. It is open to Biological Sciences, Chemistry BA, and Biomedical Engineering majors on the four-year, one-co-op track. Applicants need at least a 3.5 weighted GPA and 1420 SAT or 32 ACT, and test-optional applicants are not eligible for this combined program.

Drexel’s program page says acceptance into the College of Medicine is not guaranteed and lists undergraduate continuation requirements including maintaining at least a 3.6 GPA in all coursework and BCPM classes, completing at least 160 hours of service, and completing a six-month co-op in research, clinical, health informatics, health law, or bioengineering.

For fall 2025, Drexel reports 2,053 total BA/BS+MD applicants, 1,063 meeting minimum GPA/SAT/ACT/deadline requirements, 120 selected for interview, 61 admitted, and 24 enrolled.

## Strategic use case

Best used in the app as:

> Private urban co-op pre-health option with active BS+MD early assurance, but high sticker price and program complexity.

Drexel is useful for comparing:

* Private urban pre-health vs. public urban Temple
* Co-op model vs. traditional undergraduate schedule
* BS+MD early assurance vs. other direct/linkage programs
* Philadelphia clinical/research ecosystem vs. cost
* Health-tech/biomedical engineering/co-op fallback value

## Watch-outs

* Very high sticker price unless aid changes the math.
* Quarter/co-op structure complicates cost and timeline modeling.
* BA/BS+MD is highly competitive and not guaranteed continuation into med school.
* Need to distinguish Drexel undergraduate path from Drexel College of Medicine path.

## Preliminary score notes

* Prestige band: 3
* Premed ecosystem: strong/interesting, especially because of co-op and early assurance
* Value: aid-dependent
* Clinical/research access: strong
* Fit question: Does Lucy like urban Philadelphia and a co-op-driven academic calendar?

# What this batch teaches

This batch starts to show why the app needs multiple lenses.

## Cost lens

For a NJ family, many “reasonable regional publics” become expensive once priced as out-of-state options:

* Pitt OOS Arts & Sciences estimated rough annual cost is about $62K before aid.
* UMD nonresident on-campus estimated cost is about $65.5K.
* Delaware nonresident on-campus estimated cost is about $65.9K.
* Stony Brook OOS estimated cost is about $58.8K.
* Binghamton OOS estimated cost is about $55.7K.

That makes Rutgers, TCNJ, Rowan, NJIT, and other NJ options important baselines.

## Premed ecosystem lens

The strongest “premed ecosystem” candidates in this batch appear to be:

* Pitt
* Stony Brook
* Temple
* Drexel
* UMD

## Program/pathway lens

The most interesting special-pathway schools in this batch are:

* Pitt: School of Medicine Guaranteed Admission Program
* Temple: Pre-Med Health Scholars / LKSOM linkage plus accelerated health-profession programs
* Drexel: BA/BS+MD Early Assurance
* Delaware: health-professions advising and possible health-sciences ecosystem
* Stony Brook: strong pre-health and medical-center ecosystem

## Parent ROI lens

Several of these schools may be excellent, but at full OOS/private prices they need to clear a high bar:

> What does this school buy Lucy that Rutgers, TCNJ, Rowan, NJIT, or Stevens do not?

That does not mean “don’t apply.” It means the app should flag them as strategically interesting but cost-sensitive.
